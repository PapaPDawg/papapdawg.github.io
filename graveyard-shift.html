Graveyard Shift

A cozy narrative management game built in Unreal Engine 5 with a focus on modular gameplay systems, event-driven architecture, and emotionally driven storytelling.

Overview

Graveyard Shift is a cozy narrative management game being developed in Unreal Engine 5. Players work the evening shift as a cemetery caretaker, balancing routine maintenance with supernatural encounters and character-driven storytelling.

While the game focuses on emotionally reflective gameplay, the project also serves as a demonstration of my gameplay programming philosophy: modular systems, event-driven architecture, and clear ownership of gameplay responsibilities. Rather than building isolated mechanics, each feature is designed as a reusable system that can grow alongside the project.

Gameplay Loop

Start the Shift

Check in with Harold and receive your responsibilities for the evening.

Maintain the Grounds

Complete daily tasks such as removing trash, pulling weeds, and cleaning neglected graves throughout the cemetery.

Explore the Cemetery

Spend time exploring the cemetery after completing your work. Future updates will introduce lingering spirits, narrative encounters, and relationship-driven storylines.

Report Back

Return to Harold to review your progress, complete assignments, and collect your pay.

End the Day

Once Grandma arrives, leave the cemetery, reflect on the night's events during the drive home, and prepare for the next shift.

Features & Screenshots

Current gameplay footage and screenshots will be added as development continues. The current vertical slice includes the complete workday gameplay loop, dialogue framework, chore systems, HUD, day progression, and the newly developed multi-level transition architecture.

Systems

  • Reusable Tap / Hold / Talk interaction framework
  • Randomized daily trash, weed, and grave-cleaning assignments
  • Dynamic grave state and completion tracking
  • Reusable branching dialogue system with interruption handling
  • Automatic time pausing and movement locking during dialogue
  • Multi-day work loop with daily pay and progression
  • Dynamic HUD with money, day, time, and objective tracking
  • 12-hour in-game clock with overtime warning and automatic shift ending
  • Reusable material-driven iris transition system
  • Event dispatchers for animation-complete gameplay sequencing
  • Event-driven Grandma arrival and departure architecture
  • Cross-level transition between the graveyard and drive-home scene
  • Manager-based orchestration through BP_GrandmaDriveManager
  • Modular vehicle actor with player, Grandma, and camera attachment points
  • Stored actor references for clean manager-to-actor communication

Engineering Highlights

  • Designed a manager-driven gameplay architecture that separates orchestration from actor behavior.
  • Implemented event-driven Blueprint communication using dispatchers rather than timing-based delays.
  • Developed reusable interaction, dialogue, HUD, and transition systems.
  • Architected cross-level gameplay flow using dedicated transition levels and manager classes.
  • Prioritized modular, maintainable systems before visual polish to support long-term scalability.
